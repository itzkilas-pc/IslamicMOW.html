Fajr

2 Sunnah + 2 Fardh (obligatory)

Before sunrise, at dawn

Why this time?

Begins the day with spiritual grounding before worldly distractions. The world is still, allowing for deep reflection and connection with the Creator.

After the sun passes its zenith (midday)

Why this time?

Breaks the momentum of daily work to recenter and realign priorities. Prevents becoming consumed by worldly affairs during the busiest part of the day.

Immediately after sunset

Why this time?

Marks the transition from day to night with gratitude for the day's provisions. The swift timing (narrow window) teaches punctuality and discipline.

After twilight disappears (nightfall)

Why this time?

Closes the day with reflection and seeking forgiveness before sleep. Performed in darkness, symbolizing humility and the unseen connection with God.
